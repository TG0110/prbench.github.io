Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models have made great progress in grounding tasks, yet existing methods still struggle to unify precise localization and complex reasoning. PR-Bench is a new referring expression comprehension benchmark designed to explicitly evaluate the perception-reasoning gap.

PR-Bench contains 6,000 high-resolution query-box pairs across six mutually exclusive subcategories: Attribute, Position, Interaction, Relation, Commonsense, and Rejection. The benchmark separates visual-cue perception from compositional reasoning and exposes where models fail under dense objects, small regions, semantic relations, and absent references.

Built from FineHARD images and a human-verified annotation pipeline, PR-Bench serves as a formal diagnostic leaderboard for REC systems.

Construction Pipeline

1. Image filteringFineHARD images are filtered by resolution range, detected-box distribution, and scene richness.
2. Structured parsingQwen2.5-VL-72B decomposes each scene into object categories, attributes, relations, and short descriptions.
3. Region groundingGrounding DINO grounds the parsed descriptions, while images with too few categories or objects are discarded.
4. Region correctionEach candidate box is converted into a checklist and retained only when all object properties are visually consistent.
5. Task-aware expression generationRule-based selection assigns seed objects to six subcategories, then Qwen2.5-VL-72B rewrites task-specific expressions.
6. Final verificationConsistency, uniqueness, and multi-round expert review remove hallucinated, ambiguous, or imprecise query-box pairs.

Analysis

1
Overall capability gap

Models drop substantially on PR-Bench: none exceeds 72% Accp, and AccRC is 6.02% lower than AccAPI on average.

2
Perceptual difficulty drivers

Same-category distractors, smaller target area, and longer object-hop chains reduce accuracy by 20.77%, 25.64%, and 10.88% on average.

3
Reasoning and hallucination

Thinking mode improves Relation and Commonsense, while rejection remains vulnerable to localization hallucination under dense visual references.

Benchmark Difficulty Profile

PR-Bench emphasizes small or marginally visible targets and dense, discriminative visual cues. Compared with existing REC benchmarks, its long expressions are designed to carry useful grounding evidence rather than context-irrelevant description.

Perceptual-Aware Evaluation

Performance decreases as scenes become visually harder: more same-category distractors, smaller boxes, and additional reference hops all degrade grounding. In the hardest bins, only Qwen3-VL-8B remains above 60% accuracy in the paper analysis.
